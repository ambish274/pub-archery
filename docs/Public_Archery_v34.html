Help & Guide

Welcome to ArcheryKeeper! 🎯

This app is designed to make keeping your archery scores as easy as tapping a button. Here is your quick-start guide.

Good to Know: How Your Score is Saved

You do not need to create an account, download an app from an app store, or manually hit a "save" button. ArcheryKeeper runs right in your browser and automatically saves every single tap directly to your phone's memory. If you accidentally close your browser, switch to a different app, or lose cell service, don't panic! Just open the page back up, and you will be dropped right back into your round exactly where you left off.

1. Setting Up Your Round

When you first open the app, you will be on the Round Setup screen.

  • Select Round Type: Tap the dropdown menu that says "-- Please Select Round Type --" and choose the game you are playing (like IBO, ASA, Vegas, or NFAA). Note: If you want to use Fred Bear scoring or build your own specific round, choose those options and see the Mini-Guides at the bottom of these instructions!
  • Number of Targets: For some rounds, a plus (+) and minus (-) button will appear. Tap these to set how many targets you are shooting today.
  • Starting Target # (Shotgun Start): If you are shooting a 3D round, you can specify exactly which target you are starting at on the course. The app will adapt the numbering for you.
  • Add Shooters: Tap the "Shooter Name" box, type a name, and tap the green + Add Shooter button. Repeat this for everyone in your group.
  • Tap the big green START ROUND button at the bottom.

2. Keeping Score

Once the round starts, scoring is designed to be big and easy to read.

  • Entering Scores: You will see big, colored number buttons at the bottom of the screen. Simply tap the score for each arrow you shoot.
  • Switching Shooters: The app will automatically move to the next person once you enter all your arrows for that target (called an "End"). If you need to skip someone manually, tap the blue Next > button.
  • Watching the Score: Your total score is clearly displayed at the top left, and your score for the current target is on the top right.

3. Fixing Mistakes

Mistakes happen! Here are two easy ways to fix them:

  • The Quick Fix: If you just tapped the wrong score, tap the dark Undo button in the middle of the screen. It will erase your last tap.
  • Fixing Past Scores:
    1. Tap the Scorecard icon (it looks like a little list/table) at the very top right of your screen.
    2. Find the wrong score on the table and tap on the tiny colored arrow numbers.
    3. A new window will pop up letting you replace the wrong score with the right one. Tap Done to save.

4. Editing Names or Player Order

  • Need to fix a spelling mistake? Tap the Scorecard icon at the top right, then tap the little pencil icon next to the shooter's name.
  • Need to change who shoots first? On the main scoring screen, tap the Users icon (it looks like two people) at the top right. Use the up and down arrows to change the order.

5. Viewing and Sharing Scorecards

When you finish your round, or whenever you want to check the full math:

  • Tap the Scorecard icon at the top right.
  • You will see a clean table of everyone's scores. Tap a person's name at the top to see their specific scorecard.
  • To Share: Scroll to the bottom of the scorecard.
    • Tap Copy to Share to copy the scores as plain text to paste into an email or message.
    • Tap Share Image to open a picture of your scorecard. From there, you can tap Share to send it directly to your friends, or use the Save/Copy buttons to keep it for your records.

6. Starting a New Game

Ready for a new round? Tap the Reset icon (the circular arrow) at the very top right of your screen. This will clear out the current game so you can start fresh.

🐻 Fred Bear 3D Scoring Mini-Guide

Designed primarily for traditional shooters, the Fred Bear scoring system rewards ethical, high-stakes hunting accuracy and penalizes poor shots.

The Default Rules: When you select "Fred Bear 3D" from the Round Setup menu, the app automatically sets up the traditional scoring standard: 5 points for a clean hit in the kill zone (Vitals), -5 points for a wounding body hit, and 0 points for a complete miss.

Customizing the Rules: If your club plays by different rules, you can easily change the math right on the Setup screen. When you select Fred Bear 3D, a special orange menu will appear.

  • Adjust Values: Tap the numbers under Vital / Kill, Body / Wound, or Miss to type in your own custom point values.
  • Adding an X-Ring/Heart: If your shoot awards bonus points for hitting a tiny center ring, just type that point value into the "X (Opt)" or "Heart (Opt)" box. The app will automatically adapt and build a custom keypad for you. If you don't use these bonus rings, just leave them blank for a clean 3-button layout.

🛠️ Custom Round Builder Mini-Guide

If you are shooting a unique format—like mixing different distances at a field event or changing scoring rules halfway through—the Custom Round Builder lets you create exactly what you need.

How to build your round:

  • On the Setup screen, tap the Round Type dropdown and select Custom Round Builder...
  • A blue "Custom Round" box will appear. By default, it gives you one "Target/Distance" to set up.
  • Name it: Tap where it says "Target/Distance 1" and type a name (for example, "20 Yard Field Target" or "3D Target 1").
  • Choose the Rules: Tap "Choose Scoring Rules" to pick the point system for this specific target (like IBO 11-10-8-5-0 or a standard 5-0).
  • Set the Math: Type in the Total Ends (how many times you are shooting this target) and Arr/End (how many arrows you shoot each time). The app will automatically calculate your X-ring value, but you can change it at the bottom if needed.
  • Add More: If you are moving to a different target, distance, or scoring style next, tap the + Add Another Target/Distance button and repeat the steps above.

Managing your custom list:

  • Made a mistake? Tap the red Trash Can icon to delete a target.
  • Need to reorder them? Use the Up and Down Arrows to shift the target order around before you start shooting.
  • Once everything looks right, add your shooters and hit START ROUND! The app will automatically switch your scoring buttons and rules as you move from one custom target to the next.
